# **ACE_IP_Statement.md**  
*Version 1.0 — Institutional Intellectual Property Statement*  

## **1. Purpose of This Statement**  
This document defines the intellectual property status of the ACE Ontology, the ACE Methodology, and all associated constitutional documents published by the originator and maintained by the ACE Foundation.

It establishes:

- authorship  
- ownership  
- immutability  
- permitted use  
- prohibited use  
- licensing boundaries  
- institutional protections  

This statement applies to all documents in this repository unless explicitly stated otherwise.

---

## **2. Authorship and Ownership**  
All documents in this repository are **original authored works** created by the originator.

The ACE Foundation acts as **custodian**, not author.

Ownership of:

- the ACE Ontology  
- the ACE Methodology  
- the ACE Metadata Standard  
- the ACE Market Model  
- all constitutional documents  

remains with the originator unless explicitly transferred by written agreement.

---

## **3. Copyright Protection**  
The ACE Ontology and all associated documents are protected under:

- international copyright law  
- the Berne Convention  
- applicable national legislation  

GitHub publication provides:

- cryptographic timestamp  
- immutable revision history  
- global transparency  
- legally recognized proof of priority  
- establishment of prior art  

These protections apply regardless of jurisdiction.

---

## **4. Ontological Integrity and Immutability**  
The ACE Ontology is **immutable**.

It may not be:

- altered  
- forked  
- extended  
- reinterpreted  
- repackaged  
- embedded into derivative ontologies  

without explicit written permission from the originator.

Any attempt to create derivative ontologies constitutes an infringement of intellectual property rights.

---

## **5. Public Access vs. Public Rights**  
The ACE Ontology is:

- **free to read**  
- **free to reference**  
- **free to cite**  

The ACE Ontology is **not**:

- free to modify  
- free to fork  
- free to implement  
- free to embed in commercial systems  
- free to use as a basis for derivative standards  

Public access does **not** imply public rights.

---

## **6. H‑Class Implementation Rights**  
The H‑Class (Handling Layer) implementation — including but not limited to:

- ACE Engine  
- ledger execution logic  
- MRV integration modules  
- settlement logic  
- identity and account handling  
- certificate lifecycle logic  

is **proprietary** and remains under the exclusive ownership of the originator.

No rights to implement H‑Class are granted by reading or referencing this repository.

Implementation rights may only be granted through:

- explicit license  
- written agreement  
- formal transfer  

No implied license exists.

---

## **7. Implementation Neutrality**  
The ACE Ontology defines:

- structure  
- domains  
- flows  
- institutional logic  

It does **not** define:

- algorithms  
- parameters  
- code  
- execution models  

Any implementation must:

- follow the ontology  
- remain deterministic  
- remain auditable  
- remain explainable  

But implementation itself is **not permitted** without license.

---

## **8. Use in Research, Regulation, and Policy**  
Regulators, researchers, and public institutions may:

- reference the ontology  
- analyze the ontology  
- cite the ontology  
- use the ontology for academic or policy evaluation  

They may **not**:

- implement ACE  
- create derivative systems  
- embed ACE logic into operational frameworks  
- create competing ontologies based on ACE  

without explicit written permission.

---

## **9. Enforcement and Legal Standing**  
The originator retains full rights to enforce:

- copyright  
- authorship  
- ontological integrity  
- implementation restrictions  

against any unauthorized use.

GitHub’s immutable revision history serves as:

- evidence of authorship  
- evidence of priority  
- evidence of originality  
- evidence of infringement  

in any legal context.
